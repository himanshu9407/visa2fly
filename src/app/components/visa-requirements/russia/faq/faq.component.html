It is possible to apply for an e-visa for Russia. However, they are only for three regions (The Far East, St Petersburg and Leningrad region, and Kaliningrad). There are different e-visas for each region, therefore you will not be able to travel to the other regions without a full Russian visa. For a full Russian visa, you will need to go manually, not through an e-visa. The electronic visas are also only available for a short period of time, eg. 8 days.

If you hold a normal (and not a diplomatic) passport, you will have to apply for a visa before your trip. Visa on arrival is not available for this country as of yet.

The signature used on the passport, and the one used on the application form must be identical. Otherwise, the application you submit to the Russian Embassy will be considered invalid.

The online form can be found at this link: visa.kdmid.ru. You will need to fill it out, then print, sign and attach a photograph with it. While completing the application form, you must choose the correct visa application centre which you will be applying to. This is important to note.

Your letter of invitation will have to contain information on the individual applicant, as well as the company. For the individual, their full name, date of birth, sex, citizenship, phone number and passport details must be submitted. Their duration and purpose of visit, number of entries granted by the visa must also be shown. If applicable, the names and dates of birth of minor children accompanying the person invited must also be shown. For the hosting company (who is issuing the invitation), the full name of the organization, its registered and actual addresses, its individual taxpayer identification number and state registration number must be provided. The first name, surname and designation of the person signing the invitation, along with their contact telephone number and the organization’s official seal is also necessary.
