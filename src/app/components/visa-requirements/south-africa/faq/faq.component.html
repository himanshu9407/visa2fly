Your visa is proof that your application has been reviewed at a South African embassy, mission or consulate, and a consular officer has deemed you eligible to enter the country for a set purpose. The visa will allow you to exit your country and travel to a South African port of entry. Here, an immigration official will determine if you are allowed to enter or not, and for how long you can stay for that particular visit.

Your visa’s period of validity is calculated from the date of entry into South Africa. It will be set out under the heading "conditions" on the visa label. This is why you need to make sure that you apply for the correct visa/permit. You may be refused entry if your intentions do not match those on the visa. The stay period depends on your purpose and how long it will take to complete your itinerary - it varies from person to person, but is stated clearly in your visa label.
