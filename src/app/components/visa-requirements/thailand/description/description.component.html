Destination Thailand

Prior to the global pandemic, Thailand was one of the top tourist destinations in the world. This was true for Indian and International travellers as well. In 2019 almost 40 million people applied for the Thailand Visa! Out of these Thailand Visa for Indians accounted for almost 4.75% of the total visas. Thus a whopping 1.90 million Indian tourists visited Thailand. With the roll-out of the Covid-19 vaccine globally, Thailand tourism will likely get an immense boost. At the time of writing this (December 2020) the Thailand Minister for Sports and Tourism, Pipat Ratchakitprakan, has announced that Thailand’s tourism is close until the second quarter of 2021 i.e. till March 2021.

This means that other Thailand tourist visas, such as Thailand e-Visa and Thailand visa-on-arrival, apart from the new Thailand TR and Thailand STV visa will still be on hold for now. This move thwarted the hopes of a turnaround in the tourism industry for the 2020/2021 high season. We hope that once tourism restarts in 2021 it will show great revival with an anticipation of getting close to pre- covid levels of tourists into Thailand by the winter season of 2021.

Covid-19 Travel Plans?

Once travel resumes to Thailand, tourists are still likely to take all standard precautions required for the Covid-19. These may include

  • standard tests for Covid-19 such as the COVID 19 PCR tests.
  • fit to fly Certificate issued by relevant authority and the COVID-19 medical test result issued no longer than 72 hours before the date of travel
  • This certificate may also be necessary at the airline’s check-in counter for a boarding pass to be issued.
  • Travel Health Insurance Policy covering treatment and medical expenses related to COVID-19 with a minimum coverage of 100,000 USD.

In addition to the above there may also be a mandatory quarantine for up to 14 days. However details of these requirements will soon be updated by the Thai Government so check this section again prior to your travels.

Thailand - a popular tourist destination for Indians

What makes Thailand one of the favourite destinations for Indian tourists is the short journey, multiple low cost flights and an easy process to get a Thai tourist visa specially with the Thailand Visa on arrival for Indian tourists. Thailand has also started an easy process to apply for Thailand Visa Online. Thailand offers something for everyone. From beaches, malls, the amazing nightlife and plenty of serene temples there is a plethora of things to do in Thailand. The most common destination to visit within Thailand are Bangkok, Pattaya, Krabi, Phuket, Phi Phi Islands and Koh Samui.

Top 3 Places to Visit

  1. Bangkok - The city that never sleeps - Bangkok is the capital of Thailand and is a culmination of the modern world and ancient charm. Bangkok has many skyscrapers, some of which you can find next to the most beautiful temples and monasteries. There are plenty of luxury malls and shopping centres which compete with the street bazaars and floating markets. Bangkok is also known for it's nightlife which has everything from flashy nightclubs, go-go bars, bright neon light signs and cheap drinks.
  2. Pattaya - Pattaya is notoriously known as the Sex capital of Thailand. However, over the years Pattya has become known for much more than just a party hub - it has pristine beaches and islands and offers a variety of watersports, excellent restaurants offering world cuisine, abundant cafes making it ideal for all types of tourists from family and friends to couples. The nightlife in Pattaya still stands out and is famous for being loud and flashy. The most characteristic place is the Walking Street which has a host of bars and pubs with bright street lights and women standing along the streets. Cabaret shows such as the Alcazar Show and Tiffany's Cabaret Show also are popular tourist attractions during the night. Located within a two hours drive distance from Bangkok, most tourists to Pattaya come via trains, buses or cars from the capital.
  3. Phuket - Phuket is an island in Thailand (the largest in fact) and has something to offer for everyone. The best way to reach Phuket is via Bangkok landing at the Phuket International Airport. Phuket is one of the most popular destinations within Thailand for International and Indian tourists. The pristine white sand beaches, offshore islands, upbeat markets (night market, indy market, Sunday Walking market), a buzzing nightlife and thrilling watersports are characteristic of Phuket.
