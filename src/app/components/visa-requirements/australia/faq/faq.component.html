No, you cannot work on an Australian visitor visa. You cannot work even on an ETA. You will need to get an Australia working visa. This is separate from any other visa. If you work in Australia on a regular visitor visa or ETA, you can be detained and removed, and may also be marked with a 3-year exclusion period (cannot apply for a visa to Australia for 3 years).

If you are employed, a copy of your salary slips for the last 3 months must be presented. A leave letter from the employer (that approves or sanctions your leave) must also be submitted. This must be on the company’s letterhead with an authorized signature.

If you are self-employed, proof of the company’s registration certificate must be shown. This could be in the form of a MOA, Shop ACT, etc.

If you have already retired, proof of the same must be shown.

If you are a student, a copy of your NOC (No Objection Certificate) letter/ID card/ bona fide letter from your respective school or college must be submitted when you apply for an Australia e-visa online.

Yes. The tourist visa is Subclass 600 of the visitor visas to Australia. You will need to apply for this when you are outside Australia (a few weeks before arriving).

The stay period depends on the documents you submit, and the time you ask for. Options are between 3, 6 or 12 months. You will be granted a stay period based on which duration the Immigration Office thinks you are eligible for. If you are an eligible parent of an Australian citizen, you may be granted a longer stay period – hence, you will need to specify this separately. Please note, this is still a normal visitor visa, not an Australia parent visa (there is no such thing, parent visas are just normal visitor visas).

The visitor visa is a broader class that includes business and tourist visas as well. Depending on your reason for travel, you can choose one of the sub-classes.
