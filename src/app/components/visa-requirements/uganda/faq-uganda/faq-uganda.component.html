FAQ's

Ugandan officers usually decide how long you can stay. Usually, this is between 2 weeks and 3 months. Since this varies based on the purpose of your visit, we cannot surely generalise between applications.

When you arrive and present your case, the Ugandan officers will present a handwritten note next to your visa stamp stating the duration you are allowed to stay.

As of July 2016, all Indian nationals are eligible for an electronic visa to Uganda. You will no longer need to go to a Ugandan embassy to get yours - instead, you can apply online with us!

If your visa or permit application is approved, this means that you are eligible for the approved visa/permit. It does not indicate that your visa is definitely approved - this happens upon arrival. At the selected point of entry (airport), or in the Immigration Office Headquarters, you will be inspected by an Immigration Officer who will determine the final approval of your visa.

If the information you entered in your application does not match with what you say to the Immigration Officer, your application may be revoked on-site. This is why you need to make sure that all the information you enter/speak is correct and true.

The supported formats include: PDF, JPEG, PNG, BMP. Your document files need to be more than 5KB and less than 250KB in size.

The Ugandan e-visa portal does not process fee refunds for online payments - therefore, the fees are non-refundable.

There will be credit/debit card payment options. You can choose one of the two. Please note, the e-visa system of Uganda currently accepts MasterCard and Visa only.

Any changes in application status will be sent to the email specified at the time of application. These changes can also be tracked through the official Uganda e-visa website under the “Applications”-->”Manage Your Applications” tab.

This modification should be done through the “Manage your application” tab under “Applications” (on the application website).

You will need your application ID, your date of birth and the passport number of the passport used in the application process.
