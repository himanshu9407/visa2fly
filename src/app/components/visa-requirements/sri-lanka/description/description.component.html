Splendid Sri Lanka!

Although COVID-19 has set back Sri-Lankan tourism, the country has always been one of the most attractive locations for tourism and vacations for international travellers. The country is divided into several resort regions which help to develop tourism efficiently. This has helped boost tourism in the country. Undoubtedly, the largest number of tourists visiting Sri Lanka are Indians with over 4 lakh people visiting in 2018 alone. The best time to visit Sri Lanka is from May to October if you plan on visiting the East Coast and if you plan on visiting the West and South Coast, December to April is the best time for you to visit. Sri Lankan culture and history too attract a large number of tourists each year!

Covid-19 Travel Plans?

The Sri-Lankan government has been trying to revive tourism for travellers and tourists while making sure that all safety guidelines are followed and that all necessary precautions are taken to ensure the safety of their citizens and the international visitors too. The government has mandated that all international travellers take a PCR test and even those who have been vaccinated must follow all protocols and precautionary rules while travelling and visiting Sri Lanka. While travelling, travellers must be mindful of the rules and make sure that they do not cause any inconvenience to the locals of the places that they visit.

Sri Lanka – Home of Majestic Elephants

Tourism is a major element of Sri-Lanka and the people love sharing their culture and traditions with the World. Due to the historical and mythological significance of Sri Lanka in India’s culture, Sri Lanka is a popular tourist attraction for Indians. Sri Lanka is the perfect destination for people who enjoy a fascinating blend of nature and history. The country is biologically very diverse and has high coverage of rainforests. These forests are home to some of the most spectacular views, animals, and plants. The country also has 6 cultural UNESCO heritage sites. Sri Lankan elephants have a very high symbolic, cultural, and economic importance in the country. These elephants too attract a large number of tourists each year because they are the largest subspecies of the Asiatic Elephants. These majestic social creatures are a sight to behold!

What Sri Lanka offers:

Sri Lanka is famous for Ceylon Tea and the lush green tea plantations that adorn the Sri Lankan fields. You can trek up to Ella Rock for breathtaking views or visit the capital, Kandy. Kandy is the most culturally and religiously significant city in the country and is surrounded by serene mountains and the magnificent Kandy Lake. You can sight whales and dolphins at Marissa Fishery Port or visit casinos in Colombo. The turtle hatchery at Bentota allows you to interact with and to watch endangered turtles grow. You can go for cruises, safaris, camps, scuba dives, and other adventure sports in Sri Lanka too. If you wish to see the vast horizon and stunning views from up above, you can take a ride on a hot air balloon at Kandalama. Make sure you research enough and decide what you wish to do or see as it’ll not only help you plan your itinerary better, it’ll ensure that you have the best experience possible.

Visa, Flights and Accommodation

While applying for a Sri-Lankan visa, make sure you first know what type of visa you need. Sri Lanka offers tourist visas, business visas, student visas, and working visas. You can find the Sri Lanka visa application online and apply for a Sri Lankan visa online too. After you have successfully filled in the required details and uploaded the documents, you can submit your application and expect the visa to arrive in a few days. The Sri Lanka visa fees may differ depending on your nationality and the type of visa you apply for, but you can pay the fees online itself. Once you have your itinerary planned, you can then apply for a Sri Lankan Visa and book your flight tickets too. The flight tickets to Sri Lanka vary according to the day you book your tickets, the company you choose to fly with, and the type of seat you take. The same applies to accommodations too. Sri Lanka has a lot of accommodation options varying from budget-friendly ones to luxurious ones too. You will not have any problem finding the appropriate accommodation as long as you book it well in advance.
