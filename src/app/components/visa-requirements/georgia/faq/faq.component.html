FAQ's

Georgia has a short-term, long-term and transit visa facility. Tourist and business visas com under the short-term category.

You will need to contact the travel agency that helped you obtain the visa. We also undertake visa processing services, so if you would like to obtain a Georgia visa, we can help you do so. If you have applied on your own, you will need to contact the Indian Embassy. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia/Embassy of Georgia in India are responsible for the creation, deletion and extension of legal Georgia visas. Extension of visa in Georgia is not possible.

There are some conditions to be fulfilled for this. You must have already visited Georgia on a short-term single entry visa in the past year. There should also be valid grounds for this request - this is at the discretion of the Ministry of Foregin Affairs of Georgia. This multiple-entry visa would be valid for 5 years, the period of stay should not exceed 90 days in a 180-day window.

It will be paid in INR, conversion rates are already accounted for. The visa fees are non-refundable.

Both the legal guardian, as well as the minor who is applying for the visa must sign. You must also create an NOC (No Objection Certificate) that shows that you do not object to sending your minor by themselves.

You will need a document to display proof of custodianship/guardianship. This could be a birth certificate (if biological parent), or any other legal document that shows the same.

In most cases, these are the only documents asked for. However, the Embassy reserves the right to ask for more documents or change this requirement, so it is dependent on each individual applicant and their unique case/circumstance.

A transit visa is a separate category by itself. Short-term visas and transit visas are different, short-term visas include business and tourist visas.
