FAQ's

After the 4-5 working day process, you will be notified by e-mail on the status of your visa application.

There are a few entry conditions for your visa. These are the conditions you need to be able to enter Bahrain after your flight has landed in the country. They include:

  • You will not be able to take up any employment (paid or unpaid) during your visit to Bahrain.
  • You will have to be able to financially support yourself (and any dependents) during this visit.
  • Your passport should be valid for the entire length of time you will be in Bahrain, or the length of the visa, (the longer duration will be considered).
  • If the validity of your passport is in doubt, it is likely that your entry into Bahrain may be denied.
  • You will need to have a confirmed onward ticket that assures your leave from the Kingdom of Bahrain after your desired travel.

    • You should apply at least 1 week in advance to your trip, but no more than 3 months before your date of travel. If your visa expires before entering Bahrain, a new application has to be submitted. Alternatively, if you have made any mistake in your application, you will need 4-5 extra days to process a new visa, so please keep that into consideration while planning.

    As a general rule, you will not be able to engage in any paid/unpaid work on any business visa. The business visa to Bahrain is not an exception. You cannot take up any employment opportunities whilst here.

    You will have to be outside Bahrain before you apply for a new visa. If you wish to extend your visa stay period, you can do so for a maximum of 14 days. This can be done at the Nationality, Passports and Residence Affairs office (NPRA) in Bahrain. Please note, you will be charged a fee.

    If you require a sponsored visa, it is the responsibility of your sponsor to arrange this. Your employer, if it is for work, this counted as a sponsor.
