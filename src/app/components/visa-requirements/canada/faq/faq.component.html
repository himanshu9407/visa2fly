If you want to apply for a Canadian business visa, you will need to be a business visitor. You qualify as a business visitor if you have come to take part in international business activities (without being a part of the Canadian labour market), or are visiting Canada to grow your business, invest or advance business relations.

You can do the following activities:

  • Buy Canadian goods/services for a foreign business or government
  • Take orders for goods or services
  • Attend meetings, conferences, conventions or trade fairs
  • Give after-sales service as part of a warranty or sales agreement
  • Get trained by a Canadian company that you already work for outside Canada
  • Train employees from a Canadian branch of a foreign company
  • Be trained by a Canadian company that has sold you equipment or services
