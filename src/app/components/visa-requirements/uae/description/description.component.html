The 7 Emirates:

Abu Dhabi

The city of Abu Dhabi is the capital of the UAE and the Emirate is situated near the Persian Gulf. This is the largest Emirate and has some of the most stunning architecture, lavish and grand mosques, the best theme parks, deserts and intriguing museums. What once used to be a fishing village, has now been transformed into one of the most deluxe, rich and modern cities in the world! Abu Dhabi is generally hot throughout the year, but the temperatures are pleasant from November to March which is ideal to visit. There is so much to do in Abu Dhabi, from kayaking through mangroves, off-roading in the dunes to witnessing tranquil scenes off the shores of the Zaya Nurai Island. The Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque, Louvre Museum, Ferrari World and the Emirates Palace are just a few of the popular tourist attractions here.

Dubai

Situated in the Arabian Desert and by the Persian Gulf, Dubai is the capital of the Emirate of Dubai. It is also home to the world’s tallest building, the Burj Khalifa and one of the world’s largest man-made islands, the Palm Jumeirah. There is so much to do and see in Dubai that you will fall in love with the place. You can visit the Dubai Fountain which lies at the base of the Burj Khalifa or even relax in a luxurious hotel at Palm Jumeriah. The Dubai Mall, Dubai Marina, Miracle Garden and Global Village too are extremely popular tourist attractions in the Emirate. You need to research and plan your trip depending on what you wish to see. The climate here is very similar to that of Abu Dhabi, so the best months to visit are from November to March. If you wish to visit the beaches, you can also visit them in October and April.

Fujairah

Fujairah is the only Emirate with a coastline on the Gulf of Oman rather than on the Persian Gulf. This Emirate does not have much to see or do as compared to the previous two but the most popular tourist attractions are the Al Bidyah Mosque, the Fujairah Fort, the Bithnah Fort and the Sheikh Zayed Mosque. The forts aren’t as magnificent as the ones in the rest of the UAE but they are still worth visiting. You can visit the Museums or shop at the iconic Friday Market too. The Emirate is usually warm throughout the year but the temperatures are lower from October to March. The weather too is pleasant around this time.

Ajman

This is the smallest Emirate of the 7, but it is a popular tourist attraction due to its serene and tranquil beaches and scenic beauty. It is home to many stunning beaches, shopping centres and museums for the curious minds too. The best time to visit is from November to March as the temperatures are relatively cooler and the weather is pleasant. Some of the popular tourist attractions are the Ajman National Museum, the Al Jarah Cultural Centre, the beaches and Dhow yard which is the world’s largest boat-making yard.

Ra’s Al Khaimah

Home to breath-taking lagoons, blissful beaches and ancient Arabic ruins, this Emirate offers tourists a blend of both adventure and culture. This Emirate also has the world’s longest zipline at Jabal Jais and stunning geographical expanses which you must explore. Other than the mountains, beaches and lagoons that you can explore, some of the other popular tourist attractions are Jazirat Al Hamra (a 16th Century fishing village), the Ras Al-Khaimah National Museum, Dhayah Fort and the Iceland Water Park. The best time to visit this Emirate is from November to April. Avoid visiting it during Ramadan as most of the places remain shut.

Sharjah

Sharjah is considered the Islamic Culture Capital or the Arab Capital of Culture due to its strong sense of heritage and tradition. The Emirate has over 10 historical museums, a world-renowned Art Museum, annual book fairs which attract people from all over the world and aquariums and zoos. The best time to visit Sharjah is from December to February however, these are also the high-peak tourist months. You can visit the Sharjah Desert Park, Al Qasba, the Sharjah Museum of Islamic Civilization, the Sharjah Art Foundation or the Sharjah Archaeological Museum. The museums have unique attractions and there are also many entertainment and thrilling activities that you can experience too!

Umm Al-Quwain

Umm Al-Quwain is the least populous Emirate out of the 7. This emirate too is heavily influenced by its Arab and Islamic traditions and culture and has a lot of archaeological significance. The most popular tourist attractions are the Umm Al Quwain Fort, the islands of the Emirate, Ed-Dur (an ancient city) and Dreamland Aqua Park (the largest water park in the UAE). The best time to visit this Emirate is from November to March as the temperatures usually range between 15 C and 27 C and the weather is pleasant.

Transportation in the UAE

Taxis are the most common public transport throughout the UAE. They are fitted with authorised meters and the rates are relatively cheaper too. Public buses too are another well-connected form of Public Transport but they are available mainly in the Emirates of Abu Dhabi, Dubai, Sharjah and Ajman. Dubai is the most advanced Emirate in terms of transportation and is equipped with a metro system, trams and a monorail system. You can easily purchase your tickets at ticket counters, RTAs, online or even buy special cards which are offered by the authorities.

COVID-19 Travel

COVID-19 has forced the tourism and travel industry worldwide to a standstill. Dubai and Abu Dhabi alone attracted thousands, if not millions of tourists each year before COVID-19. The kind of luxury and culture that you can experience in the UAE is unmatched anywhere else in the world. As populations across the world are being inoculated against COVID-19 and as the situation gets better, the UAE may soon resume travel and tourism. Necessary precautions and regulations will be put in place to ensure the safety of both tourists and citizens which must be respected and followed. If you are planning to visit the UAE, you must have a valid UAE tourist visa and you also are required to submit a negative COVID-19 PCR test. Make sure you apply for your UAE visa well in advance and book your tickets accordingly.

Visa, Flights and Accommodation

A UAE visa on arrival for Indians is not yet permitted, a valid UAE visa for Indian nationals is mandatory to enter the country. You can easily apply for a UAE visa online, fill the respective UAE visa application and pay the respective UAE visa fees. The visa will take a few days to process and then you should receive your visa. You can find the UAE visa application online and apply for UAE visa according to your needs and purpose of travel. You must plan your itinerary properly and book your flights and stay accordingly. The UAE has both luxurious and budget-friendly accommodations too. You will surely be able to find something suitable for your travel budget. Make sure you do enough research and then book your accommodations well in advance to avoid any last-minute hassles.
