Both visit and service visas fall under the Tourist Visa category, however, the stay periods and validity of both differ from the regular Tourist Visa.

As long as all required documents are filled and submitted correctly when you apply the UAE e-visa online, visas will normally reach you by email within four working days of your application.

Residents of certain countries will have to pay a security deposit, which is refunded upon your departure from the UAE. This amount is usually equivalent to USD 272.5 + Taxes (note: as of UAEvisa.net website)

Currently, the UAE Immigration does not refund fees for rejected applications. However, security deposits will be refunded within four (international) working days of the application being returned.

The UAE authorities do not disclose reasons for unsuccessful visa applications in the case of a UAE visa for Indians, so specific reasons as to why your application was rejected are not available to us.

If you exceed the stay duration on the e-Visa, you may be required to pay fines, be deported, or otherwise forbidden from future travel to UAE

Long-term or residency visas are not issued through this system. In case you would want a long-term visa, you would need to contact the UAE Naturalisation and Residency Department (DNRD) for more information.

Anyone already in the UAE can apply for a change of status or can exit the country and come back.

Transit visas can only be provided by airlines, and are available only if an applicant has an onward itinerary from DXB (UAE International Airport) to any 3rd Destination and not immediately returning (turnaround) back to the origin.
