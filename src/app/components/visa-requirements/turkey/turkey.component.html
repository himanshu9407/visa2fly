    It is possible to attend conferences, arrange trade fairs, and hold business meetings, among other things. You cannot engage in salaried employment (i.e. get a job), however.

    One visa will not be given for a whole group - each traveler must own a separate visa. You can, however, create a group application for a minimum of 10 and a maximum of 300 applicants. This will not be one visa, it will only be one application.

    Conditions include: being from the same country, having the same kind of travel documents (i.e. all diplomatic, or all normal passports), and having the same arrival date.

    No, you cannot obtain a multiple-entry e-visa. Only single entry e-visas (for both tourist and business purposes) are issued by the Turkish government. If you find someone who tells you otherwise, please note, they are probably trying to scam you.

    No, Turkey does not give out refunds for unused e-visas in the case of Turkey e-visa online.

    If you want to extend your visa, you will need to go to the nearest Provincial Directorate of Migration Management in Turkey (once you reach).     You will need to apply for a new visa, as the previous one is considered invalid. You will not be allowed entry into Turkey with your previous visa.

    An e-visa fee is only charged to an approved e-visa. Therefore, yes, you will be refunded. However, for invalid applications (i.e. submitted, approved and then later realize some details are inaccurate), there will be no refund processed.
