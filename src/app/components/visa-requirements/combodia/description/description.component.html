Attractions in Cambodia

Cambodia is a fairyland for those who enjoy being enveloped by greenery, nature and tranquillity. The Phnom Kulen National Park, the Battambang Bat Caves, Koh Tonsay, Srah Srang and the Yeak Laom Lake are just a few of the spectacular and picturesque tourist attractions in Cambodia. If you are intrigued by history, you must visit the museums, temples and archaeological sites like the National Museum of Cambodia, the Preah Khan temple and Sambor Prei Kuk. If you wish to know more about Cambodia’s bloody history, you can visit the Killing Field of Choeung Ek and the Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum. You can also indulge in thrilling activities like scuba diving, boating and biking. While you’re there, make sure you visit the beaches and experience beautiful cultural performances like the Apsara dances too!

Weather, Transportation and Culture

The country has a tropical climate with varying war, temperatures throughout the year. Depending on what you want to see and experience in Cambodia, you can visit the country either in the wet season (June to September) or in the cool/dry season (November to April). Tuk-tuks are the main and the most common form of local transportation in Cambodia but buses, taxis and minibuses are also a common mode of public transportation. While travelling through the country locally, do keep an eye out for lush fields of paddy crops and other plantation fields. Cambodian locals are generally very friendly and welcoming of international tourists and visitors. Always make sure that as a visitor to another country, you must be respectful and aware of the basics of their culture. Seafood lovers will surely love Cambodia for its wide range of delicious, mouth-watering dishes.
