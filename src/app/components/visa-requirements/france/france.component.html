TOURIST VISA REQUIREMENT FOR France

Form

Form

The applicant needs to fill up an online form that will ask for details such as Personal, Travel, Passport, Dependant, Parents, Employment, Income and Expenditure, Accommodation, Travel history (Schengen and other areas) and other histories.

Don’t worry! An agent from Visa2Fly will come to your doorstep and assist you in filling up the complete form as per the information provided by you.

Passport

Passport

Passport details are mandatory. The passport must be issued within the last 10 (ten) years and with at least 3 months' validity after the scheduled return, with at least two blank pages. Previous Passports (if any) should be bound together with a rubber band (not stapled) and sent along with the application.

Photograph

Photograph

Two (2) passport size photographs are required. Specifications - White background, not older than 6 months (size 35 – 40 mm in width, not copied or scanned picture).

Proof of Funds

Proof of Funds

Note - All documents have to be originals, in format A4, with stamp and signature of Bank.

If the Applicant is Employed :

Employment Proof: Contract of employment, Salary slips of last 3 (three) months, Bank account statements of last 3 months salary drawn.

Personal ITR for the last two assessment years (ITR-V, Income Tax Return verification form or Acknowledgement) is accepted.

If the Applicant is Self-Employed or a Business Owner:

Registration Certificate of Business / Deed of Partnership / Proprietorship Proof or other Ownership Proof.

Salary slips of last 3 (three) months, Bank account statements of last 3 months salary drawn.

Personal ITR for the last two assessment years (ITR-V, Income Tax Return verification form or Acknowledgement) is accepted.

If The Applicant is Retired:

Last 3 months personal pension bank statements.

Persons who remain in France at their own expense must be able to demonstrate their financial resources amounting to at least EUR 120 per day / Rs. 10000 per day of their stay.

Eg - 2 people travelling together for 10 days, are advised to demonstrate at least 2 x 10 x 10000 = Rs. 2,00,000/- in available funds.

If Student or Unemployed :

Personal bank statements for the last 3 months should be provided by Parents or Legal Guardians. Personal ITR for the last two assessment years (ITR-V, Income Tax Return verification form or Acknowledgement) is accepted which should include a confirmation letter of financial support and their passports (copies).

Accommodation

Accommodation

Proof of accommodation mentioning where you will be staying during your entire stay in France / Schengen Region, that could be at a friend’s or relative’s place or at a hotel or a hostel.

Flight Tickets

Flight Tickets

Confirmed flight reservation or valid both-side flight itinerary.

Itinerary

Itinerary

Your detailed travel itinerary which gives information about your travel plans in France/Schengen Area along with tickets, places you are going to visit, your meeting agenda, tours booked in advance, your festival agenda, etc.

Employment / Student Proof

Employment / Student Proof

For Employed Persons:

An introduction letter from the employer is required on the company/business letterhead. The original letter must be signed & stamped by HR or directorate mentioning your position, duration of your service, the date and purpose of the trip.

For Self-Employed Persons:

Business registration documents confirming the business owner’s name and the date the business started trading and three years of tax returns of the business.

For Students:

A letter that is issued from your education provider, on headed paper, confirming your leave of absence.and enrolment.

For Families:

Submit all the visa applications together. Attach bank statements, Income Tax Returns (ITR-V, ITR Verification Form/ Acknowledgement) of only the head of the family, Travel Insurance (includes names of all insured ones), Flight Reservations (Names of traveling members), Confirmed Hotel reservations ( Whether all the rooms are included under the same booking and under the head of the family).

For Minors:

Students above 16 years:

Students who are under the age of 18 years should provide a copy of the student college ID card and an introduction letter of the School/College/ University. If both parents are not traveling – written consent letter of the other, legal guardian or non-traveling parent, duly certified by a public notary.

For children under 18 years of age who are traveling alone:

A written consent letter should be provided by a legal guardian or both the parents, duly certified by a public notary. In all cases – Copy of Driving license, PAN card and Passport of both parents or legal guardian.

Other Documents

Other Documents

1. If the applicant continues to travel to a third country (i.e. United Kingdom, USA, Canada, etc…) once you leave the Schengen Area, obtain the visa of the next country first.

2. Provide copies of valid UK, USA, or CAN visas (Provide any).

3. If you have been refused a visa by an Embassy or High Commission in the last 2 years, please provide the Refusal Notice or a written explanation about the reasons for refusal.

Travel Insurance

Travel Insurance

Travel insurance is mandatory. Coverage of minimum 30,000 Euros or 50,000 USD / person. All expenses which include repatriation for medical reasons, urgent medical attention or emergency medical treatment in hospital. The insurance should cover the entire stay in the territory of Schengen.

Applicants applying for a period of 6 months or one-year multiple-entry visas are not entitled to provide 180/365 day coverage. Submitting an insurance policy that covers the first journey is sufficient.

    • FAQ's

    You will need a transit visa if you have to pass through border control. You will pass through border control if:

  • you leave the airport building for any reason
  • you need to collect and re-check your luggage
  • your connecting flight departs from a different airport
  • your connecting flight departs on a different day to when you arrive
  • there is nowhere for you to stay in the airport overnight
  • you are transiting through the UK to get to Ireland
  • Note: You may need to re-check your luggage if you have connecting flights on different airlines.

    • Transit visas for the UK are usually valid for 6 months and allow multiple entries as long as your future trips meet the same criteria as the original transit trip (e.g. you cannot leave the airport if you were granted a Direct Airside Transit Visa). You will also be allowed to do a reverse journey with the visa (i.e. the same visa can be used on your return trip).

    A Direct Airside Transit Visa (DATV) is valid for 24 hours and does not allow you to pass through the UK Border Control (i.e. you must remain ‘airside’). This transit visa allows you to pass through the transit channels and requires that you depart from the same airport on the same day. The DATV is perfect if you are travelling to Europe and need to catch a connecting flight in the United Kingdom.

    A Visitor in Transit Visa is valid for 48 hours and allows you to change terminals and pass through the UK Border Control. The Visitor in Transit visa is designed for travellers who need to re-check their luggage or catch a connecting flight at a different airport (e.g. If you land at Gatwick Airport and your onward flight departs from Heathrow Airport). You will only be allowed to stay in the UK for 48 hours.
